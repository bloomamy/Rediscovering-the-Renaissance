Section 1--What is it? What's its impact?

The influence of Classicism on the Renaissance has had a profound impact on the world since the renaissance area by resurging the interests of ancient Greek and Roman’s style, theme and technique, lead to the cultural rebirth across Europe and shaping artistic trend all around the world till this day. Renaissance was a period in the European civilization, it is a rebirth of culture, economic, artistic and science after the Middle Age. (Encyclopedia Britannica, 2024) Classicism is a replication and celebration of the themes, techniques and style of the Greek and Roman art. The Renaissance Classicism is a movement when renaissance scholars mimic the literature, art and architecture of the ancient world such as Ancient Roman and Greek. (GrandPalais, 2010) Classicism played an important role in the Renaissance by reconnecting the ancient Roman and Greek ideals with the Renaissance artists and scholars. It leads to scholar start pay attention on the ruins of the classical world for new models. Classical thought permeated into the renaissance art, literature and architecture, which lead to the renaissance scholars focus more Greek and Roman idea in their works. Classicism brings a new beauty-appreciation in artistic, reuse of classical style and sets a new standard of artistic. The birth of Venus by Sandro Botticelli is a notable example of classicism influence renaissance art. It depicted Venus, the goddess of love and beauty in Greek mythology, born from the sea. In literature, classicism inspired the litterateur to references to the ancient Greek and Roman precedents in poetry and theatre. For example, “Devine Comedy” from Dante is a tri-part play structure in Greek style. It takes idea from “Aeneid” by Virgil and other classical poetry. From the view of architecture, classicism has led to the significant transformation of style to ancient Greco-Roman and other classical style. Architecture during the Renaissance period imitated Ancient Doric and Corinthian style columns, Roman dome and tunnel vault. The Santa Maria Novella cathedral in Florence well presented the transformation of style from Gothic to Greco-Roman. (Davis, 2019) By refocus and praise on Ancient Greek and Roman’s art, theme, cultural value and aesthetic idea, it inspired the innovation in art, literature, and architecture and played an important role in art, literature and architecture. Classicism has had a lasting impact on the world since the Renaissance era.

Section 2--Research Question: How does the revive of classical mythology influence Renaissance artworks?

This research question has a important significance on the understanding of history and society. The revival of classical mythology during the Renaissance has had a profound impact of the innovation and inspiration among artists of that time. Through understanding the influence of classical mythology on the Renaissance artwork it can help gain an in-depth comprehend to the art trends of the Renaissance period affected by classicism.

The classical mythology led to artists focus on classical mythological theme in the artwork. It provided rich materials, implied meaning, characters and stories for the artists to use into their painting and work, brings artists a chance to explore human emotions and moral value their artworks. Artists starts depicting gods, goddesses and heroes in classical mythology. The stories and characters in classical mythology inspired artists to create artwork relevant with classical mythology. The classical mythology effects and changed the aesthetic ideal of Renaissance artists to use a balanced proportion in their work. The dramatic background stories provided by the classical mythology allow artists to use bold and bright colors instead of softer tones of religious art. People renewed the appreciation of wisdom, beauty and strength celebrated and expressed in classical mythology. Artists derived the aesthetic ideal of artworks from the theory of classical mythology.

Section 3--Possible Answers

The revival of classical mythology has had a profound impact on the Renaissance artworks by providing a rich source of inspiration and material for the artists. Artists during renaissance period has fascinated by the charm and depth of classical legends, they started using famous stories, gods and heroes from classical mythology as the themes in their artwork. The artworks and sculptures start to get into the spirit of the classical stories. It brings the audience to various of myth of heroes and epic legends. (Dataentry, 2023) Without classical mythology, the background of the painting will be less rich and not very able to express the feelings the author wants to express. Take Sandro Botticelli’s “The Birth of Venus” as an example, without classical mythology, the painting would be a picture of a woman born from sea level. However, as classical mythology has been added, this painting will be telling the birth of the Roman goddess Venus from the sea and the birth of beauty and love. (Cooch, 2024) The stories from classical mythology brings depth and provides materials and nutrients for artworks. Classical mythology gives people rich inspiration. Artists start using its backgrounds, characters and stories. Sometimes artists will express their ethical values though the artworks by using the backgrounds, characters and stories from classical mythology. Famous artists such as Raphael and Titian also reinterpreted the myths, adapted and reimagined classical stories. For example, Titian’s Diana and Actaeon recomposed Greek mythology and incorporated symbol of Christian themes. According to the Ovid’s “Metamorphoses”， Actaeon accidentally discovered the secret bathing place of Diana, the goddess of moon and hunt while he was hunting. The skull of the male deer on the top right corner hints the fate of Actaeon. Outraged Diana turned him into a deer and been devoured by his hunting dog. Titian recomposed the myth by adding Christian themes of shame and guilt and conveyed it in the expression of the characters. (National Galleries of Scotland, n.d.) Another example, “The Triumph of Galatea”. The author Raphael celebrates love and beauty by depicting the Galatea, the sea nymph, with other character surrounded her and been carried by two dolphins. (Down, 2023) Not only painting but also sculptures. Perseus and the Head of Medusa by Benvenuto Cellini also borrowed elements from ancient Greek mythology. Perseus was the son of Zeus and Danaë in Greek mythology. (THEOI, n.d.) Cellini depicts Perseus bronze statues, standing with the head of Medusa in his hand in the bronze statue. (Harris and Drogin, 2015) The trait of dramatic in classical mythology also provides a chance for artists to create a multi-character composition artwork. For example, in “The Feast of the gods” by Giovanni Bellini and Titian, Silenus, Bacchus, Mercury, Jupiter, Maia, Apollo and other many others appears in this multi-character composition painting. (Nalezyty, n.d.) In conclusion, classical mythology played an important role in the creation of Renaissance art. It provides artists with rich inspiration and materials, allows them to created artworks with depth and medium to express emotions and moral values. Through reinterpreted, reimagine and incorporate elements from different aspects, artists gave new force for classical mythology and renaissance artworks. Therefore, classical mythology has had a significant and lasting impact on Renaissance art and became an important part of artistic creation.

Section 4--Example Works

The birth of Venus is created by Sandro Botticelli, an Italian artist. It was painted around 1482 to 1485 in Italy, Florence. It depicts the birth of the goddess Venus or Aphrodite. In the rough sea, she slowly emerges from the foam of a wave. The goddess drifts to the shore in Cyprus by being carried by a piece of shell, propelled by Zephyr. The lady on the right perhaps Thallo or one of the graces, runs and proffered a robe dotted with flowers. (Cain, 2018)

In this painting, Sandro Botticelli turned to classical mythology for inspiration and materials. Venus is a goddess who characterize love, beauty and fertility in Roman mythology. Aphrodite is the Greek equivalent of Venus. Venus is heavily borrowed from Aphrodite. (Roberts, 2023) Zephyrus was the god of the west wind and messenger of spring in Greek mythology. He was known as one of the four Anemoi, the wind gods. (Mark, 2022) Thallo, Hora of spring, is the Greek goddess of spring buds and green shoots. (Rosenkrantz, 2024) Graces of Greek mythology were sister goddesses of beauty, grace, and charm (Kapach, 2023) He uses stories and character and the meaning behind them from classical mythology to celebrate love and beauty. (Finnan, n.d.) Classical mythology inspired and provides materials, depth the meaning and enrich the creation for Sandro Botticelli’s The birth of Venus.

Perseus with the Head of Medusa is a sculpture made by Benvenuto Cellini around 1545 in Padua. It depicts Perseus killed Medusa with the help of the gods, holding her head and blood squirted from her body on the ground with a sword in the other hand. This bronze sculpture is full of details. (Victoria and Albert Museum, 2006)

In this sculpture, Benvenuto Cellini references to the story of the ancient Greek hero Perseus. The sculpture shows Perseus with the winged sandals from Hermes and satchel from Athena, which match with ancient Greek mythology, Hermes and Athena helped Perseus. Classical mythology inspired, provides rich materials and enrich the creation for Benvenuto Cellini to create this sculpture. (Wray, 2022)
